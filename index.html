Our Mission

Accountability Where It Has Been Absent

The commercial maritime industry operates under a federal credentialing framework administered by the United States Coast Guard National Maritime Center. While that framework establishes rigorous standards for mariner licensing, a significant gap exists in the verification and enforcement of drug and alcohol testing compliance — particularly across third-party consortium administrators.


Maritime Credential Integrity Group LLC was founded to document this gap through independent research, evidence-based analysis, and formal engagement with the agencies responsible for mariner safety. Our work is grounded in verified facts, not adversarial posturing. The record speaks for itself.

Focus Areas

Where We Direct Our Work

⚓
Credential Integrity

Examining how Merchant Mariner Credentials are enrolled, tracked, and maintained across third-party testing administrators and federal databases.

🧭
Regulatory Gap Analysis

Identifying the absence of a centralized national drug testing clearinghouse equivalent for maritime — analogous to the FMCSA Clearinghouse for commercial trucking.

📋
Documentation & Research

Building an evidence record through FOIA requests, incident documentation, and cross-referencing publicly available federal reporting data.

🏛️
Policy Engagement

Formal engagement with USCG, NMC, and relevant Congressional oversight channels to advance structural reform through proper institutional channels.
